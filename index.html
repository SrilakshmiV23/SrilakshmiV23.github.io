Who I Am

What I Like to Do

I am a backend engineer who got curious about AI and decided to do something about it. Here is what genuinely excites me.

⚙️

Building Systems That Don't Break

There is something deeply satisfying about a backend service that handles thousands of events without complaint. At Allianz, I spent years making Kafka pipelines reliable. I love tracing a bug through distributed logs until I find the exact moment something went wrong — and fixing it cleanly.

🤖

Teaching Machines to Be Useful

My Bachelor's project — classifying teaching styles from lecture videos — was the moment I realized ML isn't just theory. Watching a model actually distinguish between teaching styles felt like magic. Now I want to bring that same intelligence into production backend systems.

📊

Finding Patterns in Data

The Spotify project stuck with me. Audio features predicting whether a song becomes popular? I spent way too long exploring feature combinations. I like asking "what does this data actually tell us?" before touching a model.

🔗

The Gap Between AI Research and Real Products

Most ML models never make it to production. I want to be the person who bridges that gap — someone who understands both the Spring Boot microservice and the model it needs to serve. That intersection is where I want to build my career.
